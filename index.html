About Me...

Hi, I'm Valia Cantillo Pileta.

Saying that I had no coding experience before I started this transition to tech would be a lie, but the true is that I just wish I could say that my first experience coding as a university student made me love coding but it didn't. But anothe true is that I have always felt a great interest in everything related to technology. Since I was a teenager I have felt passion for everything related to typography, layout and color, elements that for me are tools to express myself artistically and communicate my vision and ideas. Reason for what I never doubt and when I got the opportunity to learn more about tecnology in my small country, I choose and course in the UCI(University of Science) in Havana, Cuba.

I love developing websites from scratch, enjoyning the whole process of translating both my own designs and the client's designs into a clean, tidy, scalable and easy to maintain and read code.I consider myself a highly decisive person and I feel equally at ease working on my own or as part of a team. My diverse experience working in both North America and Latin America makes me a strong candidate for cross-cultural collaboration, especially in the current remote and distributed team environment. My curiosity and passion for applying technology to solve various business and engineering problems make me a valuable member of any engineering team.

For the past 7 years, I have been creating and working in our family trucking company as a CEO, Manager and Dispatch, at the same time that I was taking care of my 2 babies at home. But as an always innovating person I have a desire to learn, explore, grow and be challenged and that is what I love about coding. This journey has led me to Software Development and I am excited to move into the Tech Industry with a focus on Frontend Development using HTML, CSS, Javascript.

Highly motivated, cheerful and approachable. I Enjoy taking on new challenges and learning new skills. Passion for making ideas come to life. I love coding challenges and that euphoric feeling of getting my code to work...I have heard several times that I am one heck of an enthusiastic debugger.

Currently I just finished my BootCamp in Web Development at Udemy with Angela, and I continue training by myself in the use of frameworks and other languages, while working on freelancing projects for clients.
